Are You Living on Autopilot?

We often speak about the third eye as if it were something hidden. We imagine a secret power or a doorway to another world. Yet there is a more useful way to interpret it. The third eye may be nothing more than the ability to see yourself clearly.

We move through life on automatic, react before we think, follow habits we never chose, repeat stories about ourselves without asking whether those stories are true. We become so close to our thoughts that we cannot tell where the thought ends and where we begin.

The third eye begins with a small shift. Instead of being inside every thought, we start looking at our thoughts. Instead of becoming every emotion, we start noticing our emotions. We watch anger rise, fear appear, excitement take hold. The feeling is still there, but now there is space between us and it.

This changes everything.

When we can see our own mind at work, we begin to notice patterns. We see one belief lead to another. We see how old fears shape new choices. We see how our attention is pulled toward what confirms what we already think. What once felt like reality starts to look more like a picture that our mind has painted.

The world does not change. Our view of it does.

This deeper kind of seeing is not about escaping life. It is about entering life with open eyes. We become less trapped by impulse, less controlled by old habits, more able to choose our response instead of acting without thought.

We discover this through quiet reflection, writing, long walks, hard conversations, or moments of failure.

There is also freedom in realizing that not every thought deserves trust. A thought can be loud and still be wrong. A feeling can be strong and still pass away. When we understand this, we stop treating every mental event as a command. We become an observer as well as a participant.

We see things with greater clarity, recognize the stories, assumptions, and reactions that shape our experience. We learn to question them and understand them.

The third eye is about looking beyond the first version of ourself that appears in our mind. It is the ability to stand back, pay attention, and see clearly. This simple act can change our life.
